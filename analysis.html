What it is: Under IND AS 116 (effective April 2019), airlines must capitalise all aircraft operating leases. The "Right-of-Use Asset" represents the economic right to fly leased aircraft for the contract term, discounted to present value.

IndiGo Context: IndiGo owns ZERO aircraft. All 370 aircraft are leased. This creates a ₹36,420 Cr ROU asset — the single largest item on the balance sheet (44.3% of total assets). This is matched by an equal lease liability. The net economic impact on shareholders is zero — it's an accounting mirror image. Pre-IND-AS-116, IndiGo's balance sheet was a fraction of the current size.

IB Significance: Analysts adjust balance sheets to remove ROU assets and lease liabilities for true leverage analysis. "Adjusted Net Debt" (ex-lease) gives a cleaner picture of financial health.

What it is: The annualised return rate that makes the NPV of all LBO cash flows equal to zero. PE firms target IRR >20% as the hurdle rate for buyout investments.

IndiGo Context: An IndiGo LBO is theoretically challenging for four reasons: (1) existing debt massive (₹51,280 Cr lease liabilities), (2) high EV at ₹1,94,557 Cr requires huge equity cheque, (3) aviation is cyclical (COVID destroyed value in 2 years), (4) IndiGo promoters (Rahul Bhatia family ~37% stake) unlikely to sell. Key risk: fuel price spike can destroy returns quickly.

IB Significance: IRR is solved iteratively (Newton-Raphson or Excel's IRR function). MOIC (Money-on-Invested-Capital) is the simpler metric: 3x = tripled money in hold period.
